Folktale: The Origin of Lake Toba

Welcome to Edukasu, a fun and meaningful place to learn! Today, we will learn a famous folktale from Sumatra Island, Indonesia, called "The Origin of Lake Toba."

Story Summary

Once upon a time in Sumatra Island, there was a handsome man named Batara Guru Sahala who loved fishing. One day, he caught a fish. He was surprised when the fish talked and begged to be set free. He set the fish free, and suddenly the fish turned into a very beautiful woman. They got married happily, but Batara Guru Sahala promised never to tell anyone that she had been a fish.

Years later, they had a son. One day, Batara Guru Sahala got angry with his son and called him “fish children.” The boy cried and told his mother. The mother was very angry because Batara Guru Sahala broke his promise. Then, the earth started to shake, and volcanoes erupted. A big hole appeared and became a lake. That lake is now known as Lake Toba.

Story Elements

  • Characters: Batara Guru Sahala, the fish-woman, and their son
  • Setting: Sumatra Island
  • Problem: Batara Guru Sahala broke his promise
  • Events: He caught a talking fish, married the woman, had a son, and broke the promise
  • Ending: The earth shook and became Lake Toba
  • Moral Value: We must keep our promises and never break trust

Activity 11 – Questions and Answers

  1. Who are the characters in the story?
    The characters are Batara Guru Sahala, the fish-woman (his wife), and their son.
  2. Where was the story happened?
    The story happened in Sumatra Island.
  3. What happened in the story?
    Batara Guru Sahala caught a talking fish, which turned into a beautiful woman. They got married and had a son. One day, Batara Guru Sahala got angry and called his son “fish children,” breaking his promise. The earth shook, volcanoes erupted, and a big lake was formed.
  4. What is the problem of the story?
    Batara Guru Sahala broke his promise not to tell anyone that his wife was once a fish.
  5. How did the story end?
    The fish-woman became very angry, the earth shook, and a large hole filled with water, becoming Lake Toba.
  6. What can we learn from the story?
    We learn that we must keep our promises and respect others’ trust.
